My Story

My journey into app development began in the 10th grade when I chose computer science over biology because I wanted to build websites, games, and applications. Although college didn't offer the hands-on experience I hoped for, it strengthened my drive to learn and grow independently. In my third year, I discovered Flutter — its dynamic community and powerful features inspired me to think creatively and solve real-world problems. This naturally sparked my deeper interest in data-driven development. After college, I joined AVASOFT as a Flutter Developer — a turning point that fueled my passion for Data Science and AI. I contributed to Herse, an AI-powered bilingual fitness app that adapts wellness routines in real-time using user mood, energy, and activity data. I also worked on Siren, an AI-powered expense tracker that uses smart analytics and clear visualizations to help users manage budgets and gain meaningful insights. These experiences opened my eyes to how data analytics and AI transform user experiences, showing me that the real power lies not just in coding but in organizing, visualizing, and predicting with data. I'm now committed to pursuing a Master's in Data Science, and to prepare, I've cleared IELTS and am learning German to expand my opportunities in the global tech space. Today, my focus goes beyond app development — I'm passionate about using Data Science and AI to build smarter, adaptive, and impactful solutions that make a real difference.

⚡ IoT & Hardware Projects

1

EV Emergency Backup & Switching

In response to the challenges faced by electric vehicles (EVs) during power depletion scenarios, I conceptualized and implemented the "EV Emergency Backup and Switching" project. The innovative system comprises a dual-battery setup within an EV, featuring a primary high-capacity battery and a secondary backup battery with solar-powered charging mechanism. The project operates intelligently, leveraging sensors to detect energy levels of both batteries. When the primary battery reaches a predefined threshold, the system seamlessly switches to the backup battery, ensuring continuous vehicle operation. The entire system was prototyped using Arduino, embedded C, and various sensors, demonstrating the feasibility of a robust and eco-friendly power management solution for electric vehicles.

Arduino Embedded C Sensors IoT
